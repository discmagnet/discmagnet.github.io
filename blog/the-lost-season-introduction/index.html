The Lost Season: Introduction

March 24, 2020 Kyle Kumbier

3 minutes read

 Twitter

 Facebook

 Reddit

 LinkedIn

 Pinterest

 Email

There’s no crying in baseball!

If you’re like me, you’re incredibly sad that baseball has been halted. And not just baseball, FANTASY BASEBALL. We wait months for the draft! We finally build our teams! And now we wait… indefinitely. Who knows, the 2020 season may never come. That idea of not having something to root for is scary.

Not on my watch.

Fortunately for all of us, the newest version of Out of the Park Baseball (OOTP) was released on March 20, 2020. With this beautiful video game, we have the ability to simulate an entire MLB season. I’m sure you can see where I’m going with this. We could all use something to root for in the following months, so I intend to simulate an entire MLB regular season, export the statistics created by OOTP, and replicate the scoring algorithm of our league. The MLB regular season may be suspended, but the 2020 season of the Inglourious Batters Fantasy Baseball League will go on as scheduled!

Week by week, I will share some highlight stories of the simulated performances, the simulated league standings of the MLB, and ultimately, the resulting standings of our fantasy baseball league. The results of a simulation from a video game will never carry the same weight as an authentic MLB regular season of baseball, but there is a reason we call it fantasy baseball. Kind of like the 2017 Astros, the winner of this simulation will not deserve the official championship title, but this is going to be fun!

Notes/Rules:

  • Game Winning RBIs and Pickoffs will not be included in the scoring algorithm since these stats are not recorded by OOTP. Every other scoring category is included.
  • The only players on each roster who are eligible to score are those in the lineup (i.e. bench players do not count towards scoring). This is done to account for different teams having a different number of position players and pitchers in the bench slots. Bench players will be used in the case of injuries.
  • Injured players will only be replaced in the lineup at the beginning of the week.
  • If a player is lost for the year due to injury, that player will be dropped. Any other player injured and expected to return at some point in 2020 will be placed on the IL.
  • Each team will get as many IL slots as they need.
  • In the event that a team needs a new player from free agency, I will automatically select the top available player ranked by most projected points.
  • There will be no GS limits for pitching. It shouldn’t be an issue because I will not be rotating pitchers from the bench into the official 5-man rotation.
